Detection of Skin Cancer Risk Using Data Analytics

Project Overview

Skin cancer remains one of the most preventable yet increasingly common diseases when detected early. This project explores how data analytics can identify high-risk individuals and early clinical warning signs, enabling proactive healthcare interventions.

Using structured healthcare datasets, I conducted an end-to-end exploratory and analytical workflow in SQL to uncover patterns across environmental exposure, demographics, and lesion characteristics.

Business Problem

Healthcare providers often face challenges in:

Identifying high-risk patients early
Distinguishing between benign vs malignant lesion indicators
Allocating screening resources effectively
The key question:
How can we use data to prioritise patients most at risk of skin cancer and improve early detection outcomes?

Dataset Overview

The analysis combines multiple relational datasets:

Patient demographics (age, gender, ancestry)
Environmental exposure data (smoking, alcohol, pesticides, infrastructure)
Clinical lesion data (size, symptoms, location)
Tools & Technologies

SQL (Advanced) – Joins, CTEs, aggregations, CASE logic
Relational Databases – Multi-table querying
Data Analysis Techniques – Segmentation, correlation analysis
Domain Context – Healthcare risk modelling
Synthesis of Insights

This analysis reveals that skin cancer risk is multi-dimensional, driven by:

Behavioural factors (smoking, alcohol)
Environmental exposure
Age and demographic patterns
Symptom evolution over time
The most powerful predictors are combinations of factors, not single variables.

Conclusions

Skin cancer risk is multifactorial, driven by:
Lifestyle exposure
Age progression
Genetic background
Lesion evolution patterns

Behavioural symptoms (itching, growth, pain) are more predictive than static attributes alone
Data supports a shift toward proactive screening models, especially for:
Older populations (45+)
High-risk environmental groups
Business & Healthcare Impact

Enables early intervention strategies
Improves screening prioritisation
Supports data-driven public health policies
Forms a foundation for predictive modelling / AI applications
